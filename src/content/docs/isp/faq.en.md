# Long-Term ISP Proxies · FAQ

## 1. What are Long-Term ISP Proxies?

Long-term ISP proxies are a hybrid combination of datacenter and residential IPs. A single IP can remain in use for up to 12 hours with automatic rotation. They feature exclusive IP resources, unlimited connections, and unlimited threads.

## 2. What is the difference between Long-Term ISP Proxies and Residential Proxies?

* **Source:** ISP proxies originate from the network infrastructure of Internet Service Providers, whereas residential proxies come from real residential connections assigned to individual households.
* **Authenticity:** ISP proxies are managed directly by commercial service providers to deliver reliability and high speed. Residential proxies offer a higher level of authenticity by mimicking genuine, organic residential IP addresses.
* **Use Cases:** ISP proxies are ideal for standard browsing and accessing global content, while residential proxies are better suited for tasks requiring realistic user behavior, such as web scraping and market research.
* **Stability:** ISP proxies are typically more stable, whereas the stability of residential proxies can vary depending on the availability of the host peer's connection.

## 3. Which protocols do Long-Term ISP Proxies support?

Long-term ISP proxies support both **HTTP** and **SOCKS5** protocol types.

## 4. What authentication modes do Long-Term ISP Proxies support?

They support **Username & Password Authentication**.

## 5. How do Long-Term ISP Proxies work?

Similar to other proxy servers, long-term ISP proxies forward your web requests using a different IP address while keeping your actual IP address hidden. Long-term ISP proxies utilize Autonomous System Numbers (ASNs) belonging to consumer Internet Service Providers, but they eliminate the downside of relying on the unstable internet connections of real end-user devices.
